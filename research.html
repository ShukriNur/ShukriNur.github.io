Research

A compilation of my work on the social determinants of health — where people live, what shapes their access to food and care, and how health data systems can either surface or hide inequity. Each project is tagged with the SDOH domain it examines.

Behavioral Health · Data Systems

Behavioral Health Data Landscape Assessment

Health Equity Council — Behavioral Health Systems Intern · New Mexico & Bernalillo County · 2026

A statewide assessment of the behavioral health data ecosystem in New Mexico and Bernalillo County: what data exists, who holds it, and where the gaps fall hardest on underserved communities. I built a structured inventory of behavioral health datasets, analyzed Legislative Finance Committee and Medicaid claims data, and conducted stakeholder interviews across county and state agencies.

Data & methods: NSDUH, BRFSS, Medicaid claims, LFC reports · dataset cataloguing · stakeholder interviews

Food Environment · Chronic Disease

Food Environments and Chronic Disease

With Dr. Alassad Rasheed (STAND CDC) · Ongoing

An investigation of how local food environments relate to chronic disease burden, using Census and zip-code-level data to connect neighborhood food access with health outcomes. This work asks a core SDOH question: how much of chronic disease risk is written into the geography around us before we ever see a clinician?

Data & methods: U.S. Census data · zip-code-level analysis

Healthcare Access · Insurance Coverage

Chronic Conditions and Insurance Coverage Across Ohio Census Tracts

Graduate capstone analysis (HIMS 750) · AMIA submission candidate · 2026

A tract-level analysis pairing chronic condition prevalence with insurance coverage across Ohio, built on CDC PLACES and AHRQ SDOH data. The project maps where chronic disease and coverage gaps overlap — the places where need is highest and access is thinnest.

Data & methods: CDC PLACES, AHRQ SDOH database · Power BI, SAS · census-tract analysis

Language Access · Population Health

Underserved Populations and Language Barriers

Graduate population health research · Dakota State University

Population health research on communities navigating care through a language barrier, informed by my own frontline experience as a bilingual community health worker and patient navigator in Columbus. Companion work includes SDOH data models — star-schema designs in Power BI built on AHRQ SDOH and CDC PLACES datasets — for analyzing equity at the population level.

Data & methods: AHRQ SDOH, CDC PLACES · Power BI data modeling · population health analysis
