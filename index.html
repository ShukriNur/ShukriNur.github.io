I'm Shay, a graduate student in Health Informatics and Health Information Management at Dakota State University and a Dr. Richard Merkin Scholar. Before graduate school, I worked on the front lines of care in Columbus, Ohio — in HIV case management, chronic disease patient navigation, and community health work with bilingual and underserved communities.

I study how social determinants of health — the neighborhoods people live in, the food environments around them, the data systems meant to serve them — shape chronic disease and behavioral health outcomes. My current work spans behavioral health data landscapes, food environments and chronic disease, and health equity for refugee and immigrant communities, with the goal of building health information systems that see the people they serve.
